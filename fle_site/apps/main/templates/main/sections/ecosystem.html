Breaking Down Connectivity Barriers

Open Source Educational Content

This means that all the educational content that Kolibri offers for curation is licensed to be freely distributed and remixed for your own curricular needs.


Learn, Online or Offline

Learners can engage with high-quality educational material from the internet, even when internet connection is unreliable. Kolibri will enable educators to integrate and distribute their unique educational content through an offline server that is accessible through a local network. Content and data is stored on the local server and synced with an online repository once it reaches an internet connection.


Syncing and Sharing

Here are a few ways that teachers and learners can share data, content, and software updates -- all offline.


Peer to peer sync

With the “device discovery” interface, devices can find and directly connect with each other over a local wi-fi network, allowing for easy content sharing and data updates.

Sneakernet

Kolibri is also designed for easy transfer of content, data, and updates via the “sneakernet.” Users can use USB memory sticks to sync and share content, data, and updates through an easy-to-use web interface.

Vectorization of Content

Make downloading and transferring content a fast and easy process. We found a way to compress hundreds of gigabytes of content into just a few megabytes, and this is done by converting only the necessary parts of content items to scalable components.



Cross Platform Installation on Any Device

Kolibri is an application that can be installed on any device or platform (Windows, Linux, OSX, Android). Students and teachers can use any inexpensive, low-power device, such as an Android tablet, and still be able to engage with rich educational content through Kolibri.
